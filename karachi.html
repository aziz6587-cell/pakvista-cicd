🌊 Karachi

City of Lights — Pakistan's coastal megacity where the Arabian Sea meets a vibrant urban pulse of culture, history, and seafood.

About Karachi

Karachi is Pakistan's largest city and its economic capital, home to over 16 million people and the country's principal seaport. Situated on the coast of the Arabian Sea in the province of Sindh, Karachi has served as a trading hub since at least the 18th century and was the capital of Pakistan from independence in 1947 until 1958. Its colonial-era architecture, sandy beaches, remarkable museums, and legendary street-food scene make it one of South Asia's most dynamic and culturally layered cities.

Best time to visit: November to February, when the coastal breeze keeps temperatures pleasant and humidity low. Summer months (May–August) are very hot and humid due to the city's coastal location.

Clifton Beach

Coastal Landmark

Clifton Beach, also known as Sea View, is Karachi's most famous stretch of coastline and one of the most visited public spaces in all of Pakistan. The beach extends for several kilometres along the Arabian Sea in the upscale Clifton district and is particularly lively in the evenings and on weekends, when Karachiites of all backgrounds come to escape the city's heat and enjoy the sea breeze.

The promenade along the beach is lined with food stalls offering Karachi's famous chaat (spiced snack foods), corn-on-the-cob roasted over charcoal, fresh coconut water, and the city's celebrated seafood — grilled fish, prawns, and crab prepared in the fiery Balochi and Sindhi styles. Camel and horse rides along the sand are a popular attraction for families.

A short walk from the beach stands the shrine of Abdullah Shah Ghazi, one of the most revered Sufi saints in Sindh, whose dargah on a low hill is visited daily by thousands of devotees. The combination of the sea, the shrine, and the buzzing promenade gives Clifton a unique character found nowhere else in Pakistan.

Mohatta Palace Museum

Colonial Heritage

Mohatta Palace is a magnificent mansion built in 1927 by Shivratan Chandraratan Mohatta, a wealthy Hindu Marwari merchant who used it as his winter residence. Designed by the architect Agha Ahmed Hussain in a style that blends Mughal, Rajput, and European colonial elements, the palace is constructed from pale yellow Gizri stone and warm-pink Jodhpur sandstone imported from Rajasthan, giving it an unmistakable rosy-gold hue that glows magnificently in the late afternoon sun.

After independence in 1947, the palace was acquired by the Pakistani government and for a period served as the official residence of Fatima Jinnah, the sister of Pakistan's founder Muhammad Ali Jinnah. It was subsequently renovated and opened to the public as a museum in 1999, under the stewardship of the Sindh Cultural Department.

Today the palace houses a rotating collection of Pakistani fine art, sculpture, textiles, and decorative objects spanning ancient, Mughal, colonial, and contemporary periods. Temporary exhibitions highlighting Pakistani artists are held regularly. The palace gardens, shaded by mature trees, are a peaceful respite from the city streets.

Pakistan Maritime Museum

Naval Heritage

The Pakistan Maritime Museum, established in 1988 and operated by the Pakistan Navy, is dedicated to preserving and presenting the country's naval history and maritime heritage. Located near the Karachi harbour in the Clifton area, the museum occupies a well-maintained compound that includes both indoor galleries and an outdoor exhibition space featuring full-scale naval hardware.

The museum's most dramatic exhibit is the decommissioned destroyer PNS Babur, which is mounted on a concrete cradle in the forecourt and can be boarded by visitors to explore its bridge, gun turrets, torpedo tubes, and engine rooms — an experience that gives a visceral sense of life aboard a warship. A French-built Daphné-class submarine, PNS Hangor, famous for sinking the Indian frigate INS Khukri during the 1971 war, is the museum's most historically significant exhibit and is also open for internal tours.

Indoor galleries document the founding of the Pakistan Navy in 1947, the development of the port of Karachi as the country's primary trade gateway, the role of the Arabian Sea in regional commerce, and the history of fishing and traditional boat-building along the Sindh coast. Navigational instruments, naval uniforms, model ships, and detailed dioramas of historical naval engagements complete the collection.
